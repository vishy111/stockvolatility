What’s a Bollinger Band?
Bollinger Bands consist of three lines:
• Middle Band (MA20): the 20‑day moving average, showing the trend.
• Upper Band: MA20 + 2×SD20, marking HIGH price extremes.
• Lower Band: MA20 − 2×SD20, marking LOW price extremes.

Traders use them to:
• Spot trends (middle band slope).
• Gauge volatility (band width).
• Identify overbought/oversold conditions (price touching bands).
• Anticipate breakouts (‘the squeeze’ when bands narrow).
• Target reversals (‘Bollinger Bounce’ back toward the middle band).
