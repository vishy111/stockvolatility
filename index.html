Understanding Market Volatility (AAPL)

  • The blue line shows AAPL’s closing price over time.

  • The orange line (SD20) measures volatility.
  • Volatility remains low during calm periods, but spikes dramatically in mid‑March 2020 as the market reacts to COVID‑19.
  • These SD20 surges align with the steep price drops seen in the blue line.
  • (Hover over the chart to see exact values.)
  • The purple middle band shows the 20‑day moving average (MA20), the baseline trend.
  • If the middle band moves upward, it suggests an uptrend; downward movement suggests a downtrend.
  • The dark red upper band marks two standard deviations above MA20. Price touching or moving outside this band may indicate overbought conditions.
  • The dashed maroon lower band marks two standard deviations below MA20. Price touching or falling outside this band may indicate oversold conditions.
  • The width of the bands reflects volatility: narrow bands often precede a breakout (“the squeeze”), while wide bands indicate high volatility.
  • Together, these three bands form the Bollinger Band envelope, used to assess volatility, trend direction, and potential entry or exit points.

What’s a Bollinger Band?
  • Middle Band (MA20): the 20‑day moving average, showing the trend.
  • Upper Band: MA20 + 2×SD20, marking high price extremes.
  • Lower Band: MA20 − 2×SD20, marking low price extremes.
  • Spot trends (middle band slope).
  • Gauge volatility (band width).
  • Identify overbought/oversold conditions (price touching bands).
  • Anticipate breakouts (“the squeeze” when bands narrow).
  • Target reversals (“Bollinger Bounce” back toward the middle band).
