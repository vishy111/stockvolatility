What’s a Bollinger Band?

Bollinger Bands help traders understand market volatility by showing when prices stretch unusually high or low compared to recent behavior.

  • Upper Band: marks unusually high prices.
  • Lower Band: marks unusually low prices.
  • Middle Band (MA20): a 20‑day average used as a reference point.
  • Band width shows volatility — wide means big swings, narrow means calm.
  • Prices touching upper or lower bands help identify extremes.
